Context intake

5-minute AI conversation that doubles as the first lesson. Output is a platform-owned learner passport (aka living profile) — fully visible and editable — injected into every practice session.
Design principle: The intake IS the product's first demonstration of itself. The learner experiences a well-structured AI conversation, then immediately sees structured AI context as output. By the time they reach Task 1, they understand why context matters — because they just lived it. Target: 4–6 minutes, 5 questions, no form fields.
Conversation flow
AI coach
Before your first task, I want to understand how you actually work — not your job title, but your real week. This will take about 5 minutes. Everything after this adapts to what you tell me. Ready?
Learner
Yes.
Q1 — scope and decision surface
Tell me about the last significant product decision you made or heavily shaped. What was it, who else was involved, and what information did you actually use to make the call?
Listening for: decision ownership vs. influence, data sources used, stakeholder surface, company stage. Follow up if vague: "What would have changed if you'd had more time or better data?"
Q2 — time drain
What part of your week do you most wish you could get back? Not the meetings — the work around them. The prep, the synthesis, the follow-up before anyone else sees it.
Listening for: workflow pain that maps to AI leverage — Slack synthesis, metric gathering, spec drafting, cross-functional alignment. Anchors the first-win target.
Q3 — AI baseline (behavioral)
Walk me through the last thing you actually used AI for at work. What did you type, what came back, and what did you do with it?
Listening for: prompt sophistication (task-only vs context-first), iteration behavior, use cases. Calibrates S1 entry point and hint depth.
Q4 — tool environment
Which of these do you use regularly: Slack, GitHub, Figma, Amplitude, Databricks, Zoom, Google Docs or Calendar? Which ones hold information you often have to hunt for before a meeting?
Listening for: available MCP integrations, where information is trapped, manual context-switching cost. Routes tool-specific tasks to correct environment.
Q5 — first win anchor
Last one: if you could walk away from your first session today with one thing that saves you real time this week — what would it be? Be specific.
Listening for: motivational anchor. If vague, follow up: "What's one specific output you create regularly that takes longer than it should?"

Gap detection and intervention

Two intervention moments: real-time during practice, and a post-task modal before the next task unlocks. Gap type determines intervention — not all gaps route the same way.
The four gap types
Prerequisite gap
Missing foundational concept
Learner lacks the knowledge the task assumes. Route: backward to prerequisite task or micro-lesson.
3+ attempts with structurally identical wrong approach
Copied example verbatim without adapting
Time on task >2.5× expected with no improvement
Context gap
Knows the skill, lacks own context
Understands concept but applies it generically. Route: prompt to ground work in their specific role.
Output uses "[Company]" or generic names
Answer mirrors lesson example structurally
Doesn't reference tools in their passport (aka living profile)
Activation gap
Understands it, can't start
Has concept and context but task feels too open-ended. Route: smaller first step or concrete entry point.
Idle >90 seconds before first attempt
First response is a question, not an attempt
Output significantly shorter than task scope
Quality gap
Completed but below threshold
Task done but output has a specific identifiable deficit. Route: forward with one named fix.
Binary completion but output below rubric floor
Same element missing across 2+ tasks
Single fast attempt accepted — overconfidence flag
Intervention 1 — real-time (during practice)
Real-timeProgressive hints — least to most revealing
Hint 1 fires automatically after first failed attempt. Hints 2 and 3 are learner-unlocked. The practice AI never gives the answer — it narrows the search space and always names what the learner got right before what's missing.
Example — T1, learner submits "summarize this for my team":

Hint 1 (auto): "You gave it the content — hardest part done. What's missing: who 'your team' is and what they already know. Add one sentence about the audience. That alone will change the output."

Hint 2 (on demand): "Good. Audience is clear. Last piece: tell it what format good looks like. Right now it'll pick whatever length it wants. Specify: how many points, how long each, implications or just facts."

Hint 3 (on demand): "Full structure: [Role framing — who you are, what this is for]. [Context block — audience, what they know, what they need]. [Output spec — format, length, tone, what to avoid]. Rebuild with those three parts labeled."
Real-timeName what's working before what's missing
Every feedback response names at least one specific thing the learner got right. The gap statement is always one thing, not a list.
Pattern: "Your [X] is solid — [specific reason why]. The one thing that makes this production-ready: [single specific fix]."
Intervention 2 — post-task modal (fires before next task unlocks)
Post-taskGap modal — typed by gap kind, not generic
Shows: (1) what the system observed, (2) why it matters for what's next, (3) a specific recommended action. Learner can dismiss — gap flagged in passport (aka living profile). Does not fire for pass signals.
Prereq gap — after T2, generic decomposition detected:
Observed: Sub-tasks were high-level ("research, draft, review") not specific to your deliverable.
Why it matters: T4 (templates) and T10 (skill files) build on decomposition quality — a vague breakdown produces a vague template.
Recommended: 4-minute micro-exercise before T4: rewrite one sub-task with a specific input, a specific output, and a named audience. Start or Proceed to T4 (gap logged)
Post-taskBehavioral signals (not visible to learner)
Time: >2.5× expected = stuck (prereq/activation). <0.4× = copied or skipped (quality gap).
Attempts: 3+ identical attempts = prereq gap. Single fast attempt = overconfidence flag.
Content: Generic placeholders = context gap. Structural mirror of lesson example = copied. Missing element across 2+ tasks = pattern gap, escalated to learning insights panel.
Navigation: Returned to lesson mid-practice = self-correcting (positive). Skipped mid-practice = motivation/time pressure flag.
Spaced retrieval
RetrievalRetrieval-in-context at task start — not a quiz
Before a task that depends on a completed skill, the practice AI asks one behavioral question: "Show me the prompt template you built in T4." Learner produces the artifact (retention confirmed) or can't (light re-surface of relevant task, framed as "let's make sure this is solid before we build on it").
7-day nudge framing: "You built a stakeholder update template 7 days ago. Have you used it? If yes — what did you change? If not — here's a 3-minute version to make it real this week." Signal = used vs. didn't, changed vs. unchanged. Never framed as a test.

Canonical vs generative examples: All lesson examples use canonical structure (fixed skeleton, fixed labeling) with generative surface (content fields populated from learner passport (aka living profile) at intake time, stored in passport (aka living profile), not regenerated per session). This preserves editorial control over what good looks like while making examples feel immediately relevant. The generated content is reviewable and flaggable by the learner.
Optional capstone — verified capability
Build your product intelligence system
Using all four skills, build a working AI system for your actual CoP role. Every deliverable must use real content from your job — no hypotheticals. Evaluated against your intake passport (aka living profile) targets. At completion, your passport (aka living profile) exports as my-role-context.md — your portable AI memory for use outside Skill Passport.
Deliverables and evaluation rubric
Prompt library (S1 — T4)5 tested prompt templates: stakeholder update, feature brief, user insight synthesis, metric anomaly summary, decision memo. Each includes at least one revision note. Rubric: templates generalize to a second input without structural editing.
AI context file (S2 — T6)Platform passport (aka living profile) reviewed, edited, and confirmed by learner. Test: fresh Codex session with context pasted — AI produces correct summary without re-briefing. Exported as my-role-context.md. Rubric: stakeholder section names real people with real priorities.
Connected pipeline (S3 — T14)One MCP → Codex pipeline producing a real output: metric brief, design-to-spec gap check, or PR synthesis. Simulator fallback accepted with narrated output and synthesis. Rubric: output includes a specific implication, not just data.
Workflow delta (S4 — T13)Before/after map of one CoP process: steps removed, time saved per week, explicit annotation of which steps still require human judgment and why. Rubric: judgment steps correctly identified as human-owned with reasoning.

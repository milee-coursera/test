Context intake

5-minute AI conversation that doubles as the first lesson. Output is a platform-owned learner passport — fully visible and editable — injected into every practice session.
Design principle: The intake IS the product's first demonstration of itself. The PM experiences a well-structured AI conversation, then immediately sees structured AI context as output. By the time they reach Task 1, they understand why context matters — because they just lived it. Target: 4–6 minutes, 5 questions, no form fields.
Conversation flow
AI coach
Before your first task, I want to understand how you actually work — not your job title, but your real week. This will take about 5 minutes. Everything after this adapts to what you tell me. Ready?
Learner
Yes.
Q1 — scope and weekly rhythm
Walk me through what a typical week looks like for you. What are the recurring things you have to produce or prepare — and which ones take longer than they should?
Listening for: doc-heavy workflows (PRDs, QBRs, weekly updates), meeting prep burden, cross-functional comms. Follow up: "What's the one thing that eats your Monday morning?"
Q2 — data and synthesis
When you need to understand what's happening in your product — metrics, user behavior, funnel — what does that process look like? What tools do you use, and what do you do with the data once you have it?
Listening for: Databricks usage patterns, manual copy-paste into decks, verbal synthesis for different audiences. Identifies data literacy baseline and MCP opportunity.
Q3 — AI baseline (behavioral)
Walk me through the last thing you actually used AI for at work. What did you type, what came back, and what did you do with it?
Listening for: prompt sophistication, iteration behavior, comfort level. Calibrates S1 entry point.
Q4 — tools and prototype comfort
Which of these do you use regularly: Databricks, Figma, Slack, Jira, Google Docs? And honestly — how comfortable are you with building things, not just describing them?
Listening for: available MCP integrations, prototype comfort level. Routes S4 entry point and sets capstone ambition.
Q5 — first win anchor
Last one: if you could walk away from your first session today with one thing that saves you real time this week — what would it be?
Listening for: motivational anchor. Most PMs will name a doc or update. Routes to S3 first-win path.

Gap detection and intervention

Two intervention moments: real-time during practice, and a post-task modal before the next task unlocks.
The four gap types
Prerequisite gap
Missing foundational concept
Learner lacks knowledge the task assumes. Route: back to prerequisite task or micro-lesson.
3+ attempts with structurally identical wrong approach
Copied example verbatim without adapting to PM context
Time on task >2.5× expected with no improvement
Context gap
Knows the skill, lacks own context
Understands concept but applies it generically. Route: prompt to ground in their specific product area.
Output uses "[Company]" or placeholder metric names
Template mirrors example without PM-specific edits
Doesn't reference tools or stakeholders in their passport
Activation gap
Understands it, can't start
Has concept and context but task feels too open. Route: smaller first step or concrete entry point.
Idle >90 seconds before first attempt
First response is a question, not an attempt
Especially common in vibe coding tasks (S4)
Quality gap
Completed but below threshold
Task done but output has a specific identifiable deficit. Bar: leverage + stakeholder-readiness.
Output not ready to send to a real stakeholder
Data cited without synthesis or implication
Single fast attempt accepted — overconfidence flag
Real-time intervention
Real-timeProgressive hints — least to most revealing
Hint 1 fires after first failed attempt. Hints 2 and 3 are learner-unlocked. The practice AI never gives the answer — it narrows the search space and always names what's working before what's missing.
Example — T10 (Databricks query), PM submits vague query:

Hint 1 (auto): "You've connected the tool — hard part done. The query is too broad. Add three things: a specific metric, a timeframe, and a filter (cohort, plan type, or feature). Try again."

Hint 2 (on demand): "Metric and timeframe are in. The filter is still missing. Databricks needs to know which users to count. Add: paid learners only, or a specific certificate program, or learners who activated a specific feature."

Hint 3 (on demand): "Strong pattern: 'Query Databricks for [metric] for [cohort] over the last [N] days. Flag any [threshold] change. Return raw numbers and week-over-week delta.' Build from that."
Real-timeName what's working before what's missing
Every feedback response names at least one thing the learner got right. The gap is always one thing, not a list.
Pattern: "Your [X] is solid — [specific reason]. The one thing that makes this stakeholder-ready: [single specific fix]."

RetrievalRetrieval-in-context at task start — not a quiz

Capstone projects

Three optional capstone projects — each produces a real artifact the PM uses in their actual job this week. Complete any one, or all three.
Design principle: Every capstone artifact must be usable this week. If it can't be sent to a real stakeholder or run in a real meeting, it's not done.
Capstone A
Automated weekly update pipeline
Build a reusable pipeline that takes your raw weekly notes + Databricks data, and produces a stakeholder-ready update in three formats — exec summary, full team update, async Slack digest — in one session. Runs in under 10 minutes. You never format a weekly update from scratch again.
Deliverable 1Prompt library: templates for 3 audience formats (exec, team, async)
Deliverable 2Databricks MCP query that auto-pulls your weekly metrics into the pipeline
Deliverable 3Skill file: weekly-update.md — callable in one command next week
Deliverable 4Live test: run the pipeline on this week's actual notes. Output must be sendable.
Prerequisites: S1 (T1–T4) + S2 (T5–T7) + S3 (T8–T11). Est. time: 45–60 min.
Capstone B
Data-informed communication for multiple audiences
Take a real product insight from Databricks — a retention drop, funnel anomaly, or cohort comparison — and produce three communications from one query: exec brief, structured team analysis, and QBR-ready slide summary. Each version is audience-calibrated and stakeholder-ready.
Deliverable 1Databricks query surfacing a real insight from your product area
Deliverable 2Exec brief: 1 paragraph, implication-first
Deliverable 3Team analysis: data + interpretation + 3 recommended actions
Deliverable 4QBR-ready summary: visual-first framing as interactive HTML diagram
Prerequisites: S1 (T1–T4) + S3 (T8–T11) + S5 (T15–T17). Est. time: 45–60 min.
Capstone C
Vibe-coded working prototype
Build a working interactive prototype for a feature you're currently shaping — no design handoff, no engineering bandwidth. The prototype runs in the browser, is shareable as a link, and demonstrates the core interaction. You use it in your next stakeholder or solution-shaping session.
Deliverable 1One-paragraph spec: what the prototype demonstrates and who it's for
Deliverable 2Working prototype: clickable, browser-based, core interaction functional
Deliverable 3Light eval: 3 things tested, what passed, what you'd change before showing eng
Deliverable 4Shareable link (GitHub Pages) — ready to use in your next session
Prerequisites: S1 (T1–T4) + S4 (T12–T14). Est. time: 60–90 min.
